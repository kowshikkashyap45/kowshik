Aryabhata - Indian Mathematician

Aryabhata

Aryabhata (476–550 CE) was one of the earliest Indian mathematicians and astronomers. He is renowned for his work on the approximation of pi (π) and for introducing the concept of zero as a number.

Aryabhata's most famous work, the "Aryabhatiya," covers a wide range of topics, including arithmetic, algebra, trigonometry, and astronomy. He proposed a heliocentric model of the solar system and accurately calculated the length of the solar year.
